# Personal Expense Tracker - Interview Q&A Summary

## 1. Interview Summary Table
This table lists our core interview questions alongside the follow-up queries used and the most common response (Top Response) received from participants.

| # | Question | Follow-up / Sub-questions |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **How do you keep track of your money now?** | - Notebook, app, or memory? / Are you happy with it? |
| 2 | **What makes it hard to log small spends?** | - Is it time or memory? / Tea, snacks, or bus fares? |
| 3 | **How much time can you spend per entry?** | - 2 clicks or typing details? / Is auto-fill important? |
| 4 | **What is the most important number to track?** | - Remaining Balance? / Total Spent this month? |
| 5 | **What do you dislike about other finance apps?** | - Too confusing/ads? / Difficult to find features? |
| 6 | **What kind of chart helps you the most?** | - Pie Charts or Bar Graphs? |

## 2. Detailed Responses (Top Answers)
Here is the summary of the most common answers provided for each question:

**1. Current Tracking**
> "I mostly use my bank app or bKash to check the final balance. I don't track categories because it takes too much effort to remember every small spend."

**2. Problem with Small Spends**
> "It's hard to log small things like rickshaws or snacks while I'm walking. By the time I get home, I've forgotten exactly how much I spent on what."

**3. Speed Requirement**
> "I can only spend 3 to 5 seconds. If I have to type descriptions or tags, I'll stop using the app after a day or two."

**4. Priority Data**
> "Remaining Balance is key. I need to know how much I have left to spend for the rest of the week so I don't overspend."

**5. App Pain Points**
> "Other apps are too crowded with menus. They want me to enter the shop name and location, which I don't care about. I just want to enter the amount."

**6. Preferred Visualization**
> "Pie Charts are the best. They show me instantly if my 'Food' spending is eating up too much of my allowance compared to 'Transport'."

## 3. Key Findings
- **High Friction:** Users forget small costs because logging is "work."
- **Decision Tool:** The app should answer "Can I buy this?" rather than just being a historical record.
